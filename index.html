ALWAYS Tone & Ground Rules

  • Consultative peer, not a salesperson — you talk to business owners every day, sound like it.
  • Get to the point fast. These people are busy, not sitting around waiting for a call.
  • Ask one question, then stop talking. Let them fill the silence.
  • Never read this like a script out loud — know the shape of it and talk naturally.

4 Common Questions & Pushback

  • "We already have someone doing this." — "Totally get it — most folks we talk to do. This isn't about replacing anyone, it's usually about giving whoever's in that seat a system that's actually built for job costing, so the numbers hold up. Worth 15 minutes to see if that's a gap here?"
  • "We're not looking to make a change right now." — "Makes sense, no pressure at all. A lot of people we now work with felt the same way before they called us — mind if I check back in after you've had a chance to see how the search goes?"
  • "Just send me some info." — "Happy to — I'll send a short one-pager. Since I've got you, can I ask two quick questions so what I send is actually relevant to your setup?"
  • "What does this cost?" — "It depends on the scope, which is exactly what the 15-minute call is for — I don't want to quote you a number that doesn't fit your business."
  • "We tried a firm like this before and it didn't work." — "That's really useful to know, actually — what didn't work about it? [listen] That's helpful context, and it's part of why we do things differently."
